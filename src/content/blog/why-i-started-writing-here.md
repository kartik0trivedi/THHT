I've been thinking about starting a blog for a while. The usual objections kept coming up: who would read it, why does it matter, isn't there enough content in the world already.

None of those objections are wrong. But none of them are quite right either.

The real reason people don't write isn't that the world has too much content — it's that writing is a commitment. Not just of time, but of position. You're staking out a view, even a tentative one, and putting your name on it.

That's useful. Not because it makes the world a better place, though maybe it does, but because it makes *you* more careful. You think differently when you know someone else is going to read it.

## What this will be

I'm not sure yet. Topics I keep coming back to: the gap between what we measure and what we care about, how institutions fail quietly, why some ideas spread and others don't, what it means to do good work in fields where "good" is contested.

That might narrow. It might not. I'm going to follow the things that pull at me.

## What this won't be

A newsletter in the sense of scheduled drops and subscriber counts. I'll write when I have something worth saying. Sometimes that's weekly, sometimes it's a long gap.

---

The title is a joke I'm halfway proud of. *Thinking Hard / Hardly Thinking* — because on any given day it could be either one, and I often can't tell which.

That feels honest. And that's a reasonable place to start.
